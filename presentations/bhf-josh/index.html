A New Relationship Balanced Housing Framework
California Stewardship Alliance 4 / 14
The state doesn't earn the right to mandate by passing a law. It earns it by showing up with the infrastructure.
RHNAThe Mandate System
The state decides how many homes your city must permit. It provides no funding, no infrastructure, and no tools to meet the number. When you fall short, it punishes you. When you comply, it moves on.
BHFThe New Relationship
Your city measures its own capacity. Your city decides whether to grow. If you choose to participate, the state invests alongside you — and you keep a share of the value your growth creates. The finish line is yours to set. The authority is yours to hold.
Sacramento has never asked what your city can carry. BHF makes that the first question.

Balanced Housing Growth Management Act  ·  California Constitutional Amendment
Capacity Before Growth.
“
The State of California shall not impose housing mandates on any community without first establishing its physical capacity and funding the infrastructure required to support its growth.
Ballot Measure Language
California's housing crisis isn't a shortage of mandates. It's that Sacramento mandates growth without measuring what a community can absorb — or funding the infrastructure to support it.
Balanced Housing Framework I · Measure
California Stewardship Alliance 10 / 14
I
The First Step
Measure.
Before any mandate, every street, sewer, school, power line, and water system is measured against a credentialed national standard. The number is objective. A court can verify it in an afternoon.
Engineering facts become the foundation. Growth decisions stop being political.
Balanced Housing Framework II · Invest
California Stewardship Alliance 11 / 14
II
The Second Step
Invest.
Before any mandate, Sacramento funds the infrastructure required to support growth. Roads, water, sewer, transit, evacuation routes. Investment comes first. That's not a regulation — that's a commitment.
A state that invests before it asks earns the right to lead.
Balanced Housing Framework III · Grow
California Stewardship Alliance 12 / 14
III
The Third Step
Grow.
Cities that certify their capacity get streamlined permitting, CEQA relief, and a revenue dividend on incremental tax growth. Growth is voluntary. The terms of certified growth reward cities that choose to participate.
The rational choice is obvious. That's the design.
Balanced Housing Framework IV · Freedom & Equity
California Stewardship Alliance 13 / 14
IV
The Fourth Principle
Freedom & Equity.
No city is forced to grow. But the moment a city chooses to add homes, a minimum share must be within reach of the families who make a community work. Not aspirational. Constitutional.
The public creates the conditions for growth. The public shares in what growth produces.
